Ultra-High-Definition Blu-Ray (4K Blu-Ray) Information

4K Blu-Ray is a digital optical disc data storage format that was released on February 14th, 2016. The case they come in is the same size as that of a regular Blu-Ray disc. To differentiate 4K Blu-Ray discs at stores, the case that's used usually has a black opaque or slightly transparent packaging format as opposed to blue for regular Blu-Ray discs. Please click this link to visit the Wikipedia 4K Blu-Ray page for more information.

4K Blu-Ray Discs
Advantages Disadvantages
Has 3840 x 2160 pixel resolution (around 4x more resolution than 1080p) Cost more than regular Blu-Ray's and DVD's
Has frame rates up to 60 progressive frames per second Have to have a 4K TV with HDR or DV capabilities; a 4K Blu-Ray player; and a surround sound system to get all the benefits
Contains 50, 66, or 100 gigabytes of storage space Not instant access like streaming services
Supports High Dynamic Range (HDR) and Dolby Vision (DV) Take up space
